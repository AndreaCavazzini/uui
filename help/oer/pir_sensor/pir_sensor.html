PIR sensor

 

The passive infrared (PIR) sensor is an electronic device that measures infrared (IR) light radiating from objects in its field of view.

All objects with a temperature above absolute zero emit heat energy in the form of radiation. Usually this radiation isn't visible to the human eye, because it radiates at IR wavelengths (700 nm â€“ 1 mm), but it can be detected by electronic devices designed for such a purpose, such as the PIR sensor. They are most often used in PIR-based motion detectors.
