DC motor

A motor that converts direct current (e.g. electrical energy from a battery) into mechanical energy (e.g. motion). The most common types rely on the forces produced by magnetic fields. Inside the motor, there are coils of wire, which become magnetized when current flows and produce magnetic fields. The fields repel and attract magnets within the structure, spinning the shaft.

With DC motors you can make wheels turn, for instance.
